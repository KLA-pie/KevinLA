Decoherence and Error Analysis of Quantum Backends

In the course of my project, I delved into the fascinating realm of quantum computing by constructing an array of randomly generated quantum circuits. The primary objective was to meticulously analyze the quantum states that emerged from these circuits, employing sophisticated algorithms to calculate the expected state vector probabilities. This intricate process involved a deep exploration of quantum mechanics principles, enabling me to predict the theoretical outcomes of these circuits under ideal conditions.

To bring the theoretical into the tangible, I harnessed the power of IBM's quantum backend. Executing the circuits on a real quantum processor allowed me to measure the results and draw insightful comparisons with the anticipated probabilities.
