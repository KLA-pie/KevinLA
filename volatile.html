Volatile Migration of Water on Mercury

Embarking on the intriguing challenge of understanding volatile migration on celestial bodies like Mercury and the Moon, I initiated a project to simulate and analyze the intricate pathways of water and carbon dioxide across the surface of Mercury. The focus was on executing an efficient computational model capable of simulating the migration of thousands to millions of water molecules. The ultimate goal was to gain insights into the mechanisms underlying this volatile migration process, particularly as it pertains to the journey of water molecules towards the North and South poles of Mercury.

Troubleshooting the model proved to be a vital aspect of the project, as the complex interplay of factors governing volatile migration required careful consideration. I encountered challenges in fine-tuning the model parameters to accurately reflect the known behaviors of water and carbon dioxide on the surfaces of these celestial bodies. Iterative adjustments were made to the simulation algorithms, ensuring that the model not only produced realistic results but also aligned with empirical observations. Addressing issues related to convergence and stability became paramount, and the troubleshooting process involved a meticulous examination of the model's behavior under different conditions.

A key aspect of the project involved a dedicated effort to reduce time complexity in the computational simulations. As the scale of the simulation expanded to encompass thousands to millions of molecules, computational bottlenecks became apparent. To enhance the efficiency of the model, I explored optimizations such as parallelization and algorithmic improvements. By strategically reevaluating data processing workflows and leveraging parallel computing capabilities, I succeeded in significantly reducing the time complexity of the simulations. This not only facilitated the exploration of larger-scale scenarios but also positioned the model for practical applications in further studies of volatile migration on Mercury.
