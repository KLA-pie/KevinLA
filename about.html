Who am I?

My name is Kevin Lie-Atjam. I am an Electrical and Computer engineering student at Olin College of Engineering concentrating in Quantum Information Systems and Education. My concentration is a combination of my interests in mathematical modeling, physics, electronics, software, and firmware. Many of my projects are centered around quantum computation, but I also have experience in Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and simulation, VLSI Design, and Mechanical Design. My areas of interest in quantum computing include Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithms (QAOA), Gate Optimizaiton, and Error Mitigation for software as well as Gate Calibration and Nonlinear Optics for hardware.

Through working on these projects, I ensure that I am always learning and growing as an engineer. With my work, I emphasize a couple of items to ensure that the project deliverables are met.

Attention to Detail and Precision: Every major iteration or design I create must exceed the specified project deliverables and must be ready for any failure modes.

Critical Thinking and Troubleshooting: When encountering some type of failure, I implement as many hypotheses and test cases as possible to dismiss the issue at hand

Organization: My documentation of my project must follow a consistent and communicatable form of etiquette as well as proper docummentation for others to interpret and understand.
