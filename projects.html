Gate Optimization of QFT on a FPGA

The Quantum Fourier Transform is a piviotal algorithm with applications ranging from quantum error correction to cryptography. This project aimed to implement the QFT on a FPGA through with a quantum simulator. My endeavor sought to optimize gate operations, highlighting a critical need in the quantum computing landscape. While the project does make abstractions about decoherence, unitary nature of quantum gates, and probabilistic behavior of quantum systems, the project demonstrates a translation that can be made between classical and quantum computers to optimize the gate interface.

Estimating Ground States of 2D Ising Model with Magnetic Transverse Fields

Pierre Pfeuty published the paper "The One-Dimensional Ising Model With a Transverse Field" in 1970 describing the lattice configuration of electrons in a 1D line and the resulting nearest neighbor interaction between those electrons. By applying an external magnetic field perpendicular to all electron spins, the ground state energy changes as a result. This project benchmarks the 1D model detailed in Pfeuty '70 by use of the variational method to then construct a 2D model of allowable spins for the electrons.

Volatile Migration of Water across Mercury (2023)

Water and carbon dioxide traverse the surface of Mercury and the Moon through a process called volatile migration. I executed an efficient computational model to simulate thousands to millions of water molecules taking paths to reach the North and South pole of Mercury.
