VHDL of a RISC-V CPU

Engineering a high-performance RISC-V CPU has been a gratifying and intellectually stimulating experience. From the conceptualization phase to the intricate details of implementation, I delved into the realm of VHDL to bring this CPU to life. The heart of the processor was meticulously crafted, with a particular focus on essential components such as the ALU, debouncer, and Pulse Width Modulation (PWM). Each component played a crucial role in shaping the CPU's overall functionality, contributing to its efficiency and performance.

In the process, I found myself deeply involved in the drafting and design of various elements, including muxes, decoders, pulse generators, adders, and comparators. These components not only form the backbone of the CPU but also showcase the meticulous attention to detail required in the field of digital design. One of the significant challenges I encountered was mitigating gate transistor delay, a critical aspect that demanded a strategic approach to ensure optimal performance. Through overcoming these challenges, I honed not only my VHDL proficiency but also enriched my understanding of computer architecture, gaining insights that extend beyond the theoretical realm.

As the project unfolded, the synergy between theory and hands-on implementation became increasingly apparent. The intricacies of the CPU's design demanded a holistic understanding of computer architecture, and with each component drafted and implemented, I found myself refining my skills and deepening my appreciation for the intricate dance between hardware and software that defines modern computing systems. The project stands as a testament to my dedication to pushing the boundaries of my knowledge and skills, as well as a tangible representation of the fusion between creativity and technical expertise in the world of digital design.
