Self-Controlled Spherical Robot

During my time working on the Centripetal Electronic Robotic Navigator (CERN) project, I focused on enhancing the software that governs the functionality of this Sphero-like robot. A major aspect of my responsibilities involved refining the Bluetooth encoding to ensure seamless communication between the robot and external devices. This optimization not only improved data transfer rates but also enhanced the overall reliability of the robot's connectivity. Additionally, I delved into the intricacies of power management strategies, implementing efficient protocols to maximize the robot's operational lifespan while maintaining optimal performance.

One of the key challenges I addressed was the reduction of latency in the robot's responsiveness. This required a careful examination of the software architecture, identifying bottlenecks, and implementing solutions to streamline processes. By fine-tuning the closed-loop controller, I aimed to achieve precise and efficient control, enabling the robot to navigate seamlessly in various environments. This involved creating a special closed control loop algorithm designed to approximate functions that adapt to the changing frame of reference and center of mass, contributing significantly to the robot's improved direction maintenance.

A substantial portion of my efforts was dedicated to establishing robust serial communication protocols. This not only ensured the integrity of data exchange between the robot and its control systems but also laid the foundation for future scalability and compatibility with additional features. The implementation of these enhancements collectively contributed to a more sophisticated and capable Centripetal Electronic Robotic Navigator, setting the stage for its successful integration into diverse applications and environments.

In addition to the technical aspects, my role demanded a holistic approach to problem-solving. Collaborating with the team, I sought to create a cohesive system that seamlessly integrated the refined software enhancements. This involved constant communication and coordination to align the software improvements with the mechanical components of the CERN robot. The project not only honed my technical skills but also provided valuable insights into the interdisciplinary nature of robotics, where software intricacies and mechanical precision converge to create a truly innovative and functional robotic system.
